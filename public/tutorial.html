Math Battles

Welcome to math battles!

At the start of the game, a goal number is randomly chosen.
Each player also starts with 3 random numbers of their own.
Players use battle cards in order to obtain the closest number to the goal number.
These cards perform mathematical operations on player numbers, opponent numbers or even the goal number.
If you're not sure what a card does, right-click it for a guide.
Resulting numbers will always be snapped to the range [-1.000.000 , 1.000.000].
While every card is applied to a main number, some also require another number as input.
The card shown here applies the i-th root to a number, where i is an input number.
You always select the main number (player, opponent, goal) first and the input last, if necessary.
The input number must always be one of your player numbers.
If you apply the card to a player number, the input number has to be a different one.
For obvious gameplay reasons, cards with inputs cannot be used on the goal number.
Your opponent's numbers are always hidden from you...
... but sometimes taking a little peek could help you out!
It tells you how close they are and how you can use cards to get in their way.
A turn can therefore be used to peek at one opponent number.
There's a small chance for a card to come with a pre-peek, denoted by a green corner.
If you use this card, you get to peek at an opponent's number first.
All that in one turn!
If you don't feel like risking a move, you can also skip your turn.
You cannot skip a turn twice in a row.
Players start with 5 random cards.
When a card is used up, a new card will automatically be drawn from the card pile.
When the card pile is empty, no more new cards are given.
The game ends once one of the players runs out of cards.
The winner is the player who has the closest number to the goal in absolute distance.
Even though you have 3 cards, only the closest one is looked at!
In the case of a tie, the player whose 3 numbers add up to the highest sum wins.
The sum is absolute, meaning only the value is counted and not the sign (+-) of a number.

Shhh... don't tell anyone this, but when you or an opponent's number is exactly equal to the goal, I heard the number's paper turns gold..!

Good luck in your battles!
